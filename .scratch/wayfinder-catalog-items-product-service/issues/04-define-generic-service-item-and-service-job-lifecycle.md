## Question

What generic service-item and service-job model preserves the useful existing dry-cleaning workflow without encoding any industry-specific assumptions?

Resolve shared service-item fields, price and variant snapshots, whether job creation is automatic or configurable, the minimal status lifecycle, due dates, notes, evidence, customer association, audit events, cancellation/completion rules, and which fields are optional for simple services.

## Comments

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: Service items have prices, priced variants, and optional estimated turnaround. Finalizing a sale containing service lines creates one generic `ServiceJob` for those lines. Immediate services may be completed immediately; longer services move through:

`RECEIVED → IN_PROGRESS → READY → COMPLETED`

`CANCELLED` is terminal. Delay should be recorded as an event and revised due date rather than a permanent primary status.

Service jobs may contain an optional due date, customer, notes, evidence, assignments, and audit events. Order lines retain price snapshots; service jobs track work, not payment.

Why: This preserves the useful dry-cleaning workflow without encoding garment- or laundry-specific behavior.

Assumptions / risk: Appointment scheduling and configurable workflows remain outside v1.
