## Question

What should marketing signup, first-store setup, dashboard, mobile, POS, item creation/editing, empty states, and navigation look like when the store is neutral and item kind controls behavior?

Resolve whether onboarding asks any non-authoritative setup preference, the Product/Service selector and conditional fields, mixed-catalog discovery, inventory and service-work navigation, dashboard metrics, route access, mobile local caching, and how users are prevented from confusing service quantity with stock quantity.

## Comments

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: Remove mandatory business-type selection from store creation. Onboarding may ask “What would you like to add first—Product or Service?” purely to choose the first item form; it must not become store authority.

Item creation begins with a Product/Service selector:

- Product: price, SKU, units, variants, opening stock, and reorder fields.
- Service: price, variants, optional turnaround, and service instructions.
- Stock fields are never rendered for services.

The current mobile Set up Item flow becomes the shared Add Item flow. Existing products migrate automatically and display as Product; existing dry-cleaning service items migrate automatically and display as Service. Catalog and Sales remain shared. Inventory appears when product items exist. Service Jobs appears when service items or jobs exist. Mixed stores see both. Dashboard metrics are assembled from available item kinds and operational data.

The mobile sale picker shows stock availability only for Product items. Service items remain selectable without an availability balance, retain the quantity control, and show price plus optional turnaround. Service-item creation moves out of the current Service Orders screen, leaving Service Jobs focused on customers, due dates, evidence, notes, and work progress.

Why: This keeps initial setup simple, preserves the current product form where it belongs, and allows businesses to expand naturally without splitting mobile into separate applications.

Assumptions / risk: Users must always be able to add the other kind later through the shared Catalog.
