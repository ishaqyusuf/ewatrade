## Comments

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: Ship through baseline audit, schema expansion, dual-read/dual-write, idempotent backfill, parity reporting, client cutover, enforced invariants, and legacy cleanup.

The primary test seam is the API-level catalog-and-sale workflow:

- Create product, sell it, verify stock deduction.
- Create service, sell it, verify service job and no stock mutation.
- Sell a mixed order, verify both outcomes atomically.
- Retry each operation, verify idempotency.

Additional seams cover migration parity, public requests/tracking, authorization, dashboard/mobile item creation, and product regression.

Legacy cleanup requires zero unexplained parity differences and no active client usage of old contracts. Brain feature, schema, relationship, migration, endpoint, contract, permission, ADR, and task documents must be updated.

Why: The highest-level sale seam proves the central invariant without over-testing internal helpers.

Assumptions / risk: Migration reporting must exclude customer contacts, private evidence, tokens, and notes.
