## Question

Which existing service-request links, customer tracking, notification intents, reports, media evidence, delivery handoffs, and offline behavior belong in the first generic-service specification?

Resolve the minimum public request and tracking payloads, privacy boundary, generic operational metrics, notification triggers, manual fallbacks, delivery integration, first-release offline support, and which provider integrations remain deferred.

## Comments

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: Preserve the existing functionality under generic naming:

- Public service-request links
- Opaque customer tracking tokens
- Request-to-service-job conversion
- Due-work views
- Notes and private evidence
- Ready and delay notification intents
- Service operational reports

Public responses must never expose private evidence, internal notes, raw database IDs, or cross-tenant existence. Manual notification copy remains the v1 fallback. Provider-native messaging and durable media storage stay deferred.

Generic service operations remain online-only for the first migration release; product offline behavior continues unchanged.

Why: The migration should preserve working customer and staff flows without expanding into unrelated integrations.

Assumptions / risk: Offline service operations require a later event and conflict model.
