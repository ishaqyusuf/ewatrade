## Question

What is the canonical domain language and invariant set for a neutral store whose sellable catalog items are individually classified as `PRODUCT` or `SERVICE`?

Resolve the canonical entity and enum names, whether stores may freely mix both item kinds, which fields are shared, whether item kind can change after creation, how variants inherit kind, and the exact meaning of “service has no stock.”

## Comments

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: Use `CatalogItem` with `kind: PRODUCT | SERVICE`. Stores remain neutral and may contain both kinds. Both kinds share name, description, category, price, currency, status, and variants. Variants inherit the parent kind. Kind becomes immutable once the item has inventory, orders, or service jobs; incorrect items must be archived and recreated.

“Service has no stock” means it can never have inventory balances, reservations, stock movements, stock wallets, reorder levels, or stock conversions.

Why: Classification belongs to what is sold, not to the entire business.

Assumptions / risk: Non-stock digital products are outside this two-kind model for now.
