## Question

How should commercial orders, order lines, totals, receipts, payment status, inventory fulfillment, and service fulfillment behave when a sale contains product lines, service lines, or both?

Resolve whether one Order model accepts mixed lines, how line kind is snapshotted, how product stock deduction and service-job creation occur atomically and idempotently, how partial cancellation/refund affects each kind, and how order payment state remains separate from service-job progress.

## Comments

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: One commercial `Order` may contain product lines, service lines, or both. Each order line snapshots item kind, name, variant, quantity, unit price, and total.

Finalization must be atomic and idempotent:

- Product lines reserve or deduct inventory.
- Service lines create the corresponding service job.
- One order total and payment state covers all lines.
- Service-job progress remains separate from payment status.

Cancelling a product line reverses eligible stock effects. Cancelling a service line cancels unfinished work. Refunds remain payment records rather than silently rewriting service progress.

Why: Merchants should not need separate checkouts when selling goods and services together.

Assumptions / risk: Partial cancellation rules require explicit line-level audit events.
