## Question

How should supplier-purchase inbound records calculate landed unit cost when a merchant enters product lines, quantities, supplier cost, and additional costs like shipping, waybill, clearing, or transport?

Resolve at least:

- Required line fields: product/unit, quantity, supplier unit cost, supplier total, currency, notes, and optional supplier SKU/link.
- Additional cost fields: label, amount, cost category, allocation method, and whether the cost applies to all lines or selected lines.
- Allocation methods for MVP: quantity-based, value-based, manual, weight/volume, or a narrower set.
- Rounding rules and how to handle leftover minor currency units.
- Whether costs can be entered before all product lines are final.
- How partial receipts affect landed cost and stock posting.
- Whether the calculated landed unit cost becomes a durable product cost, an inventory-layer cost, an inbound-line snapshot, or all of those.

The answer should include example calculations for bags/shoes ordered from China and identify the smallest MVP allocation rule set.
