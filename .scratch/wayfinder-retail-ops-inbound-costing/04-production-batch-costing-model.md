## Question

How should production-batch costing work for cases where the merchant starts with an expected output quantity, such as bags of rabbit feed, and enters raw material, transport, manufacturing, labor, and other costs afterward?

Resolve at least:

- Whether v1 supports one output product/unit per production batch or multiple outputs.
- Required output fields: product/unit, expected quantity, received/produced quantity, rejected/waste quantity, and notes.
- Cost inputs: raw materials, transport, labor, machine/manufacturing, packaging, overhead, and miscellaneous costs.
- Whether raw materials are free-form cost lines or linked to inventory products that must be consumed.
- Whether v1 divides total cost evenly by output quantity or supports weighted/manual allocation.
- How production waste, damaged output, and over/under production affect unit cost.
- How the resulting unit cost posts into inventory and later margin reports.

The answer should include a rabbit-feed example and define what must be deferred to a later BOM/manufacturing module.
