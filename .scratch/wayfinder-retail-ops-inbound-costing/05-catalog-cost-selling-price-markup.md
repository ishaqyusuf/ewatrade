## Question

How should calculated cost price, profit percentage, and selling price interact with the existing product catalog and price-history model?

Resolve at least:

- Whether inbound costing updates a product unit's cost price automatically, stores a suggestion only, or requires explicit approval.
- Whether markup is entered per inbound, per line, per product, or as a reusable default.
- Whether markup should calculate by percentage of cost, margin on selling price, fixed amount, or multiple modes.
- Whether applying a selling price should create a `ProductUnitPriceHistory` entry and what reason/source it should use.
- Whether old stock keeps its old cost while new inbound stock has a new cost layer.
- How margin reporting should behave when the same product has multiple cost layers.
- What sales reps see: selling price only, cost price hidden, or cost visible by permission.

The answer should produce rules that avoid silently rewriting historical sales or confusing cost with selling price.

Please keep cost price and selling price separate in the decision. A safe default is: landed/production cost is stored as a cost snapshot, markup creates a suggested selling price, and only explicit approval writes a sell-price history entry. Sales reps should not see cost/margin unless a later permission decision allows it.
