## Question

What should the mobile design-system playground be as a user-facing approval surface?

Resolve the playground route, entry point, screen structure, sections, sample pattern screens, light/dark toggle behavior, approval workflow, and what the project owner must be able to inspect before saying the design system is approved.

## Comments

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: Build the playground as a dev/internal Expo route, preferably `/design-system`, with a catalog home plus pattern-detail screens. The home should group Tokens, Typography, Headers, Actions, Forms, Lists, Status, Modals/Sheets, Navigation/Footers, Analytics, and Retail Ops Patterns. Approval should require light/dark review, compact phone review, sheet/modal review, and screenshots or walkthrough evidence.

Why: One very long screen will be hard to approve. A catalog plus drill-in pattern screens matches the user’s request for opening other design views.

Assumptions / risk: Route visibility must be decided during implementation so the playground does not leak into production accidentally.
