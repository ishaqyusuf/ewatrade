# Define Instructions, Evidence, Privacy, And Communication

## Question

Which instructions, notes, condition records, photos/videos/files, approvals,
signatures, actor history, customer-visible updates, notification intents, and
privacy/retention rules belong to Service intake, work execution, and tracking?

## Comments

Add optional intake photos and videos to the baseline Service workflow. Media
should attach to the Service Job or, when necessary, a specific work line—not
to inventory.

Each attachment should retain its media type, uploader, capture/upload time,
purpose, and visibility. Intake media should be private to authorized staff by
default; making it customer-visible must be an explicit action. Missing media
must never block intake confirmation.

The specification should also decide file-size/type limits, deletion and
retention behavior, offline upload state, failed-upload recovery, and whether
customer consent is required for particular media uses.

---

Instructions and condition notes may attach to the Service Job or a specific
Job Line. Photos, videos, and files are evidence references with purpose, media
type, uploader, timestamps, upload state, and visibility.

Evidence is private to authorized staff by default. Customer visibility
requires an explicit safe-to-share decision. Public tracking must never expose
private evidence, staff identities, internal notes, or raw storage identifiers.

Missing or failed media uploads must not block intake. Offline capture should
show pending/failed state and allow retry. Deletion should leave an audited
tombstone once evidence has been used operationally.

## Approved Pipeline Comment

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: Keep Offering Guidance, Intake Instructions, Internal Work
Notes, and Customer Messages distinct. Instructions, condition, and Service
Evidence may target a Job or Job Line. Evidence uses managed asset references
and records purpose, media type, uploader, capture/upload time, target,
visibility, and upload lifecycle. It is private by default; customer visibility
requires explicit publication. Offline capture supports queued/uploading/
available/failed/revoked states and retry. Missing or failed media never blocks
Intake. Operationally used evidence is revoked with an audited tombstone.

Why: URL-only evidence has no durable upload, privacy, publication, retry, or
retention semantics and cannot safely support photos/videos.

Assumptions / risk: Concrete storage provider and business-specific retention
periods remain later configuration decisions; public safety rules are mandatory.
