## Question

What mobile, dashboard, and public/customer experience makes Service Offering
setup, staff intake, quoting/ordering, due-work queues, assignment, status,
evidence, exceptions, payment separation, communication, and tracking clear
without dry-cleaning or other industry-specific defaults?

## Comments

Prototype this end-to-end simple flow on mobile and dashboard:

1. Create a generic Service.
2. Add merchant-defined offerings/options and prices.
3. Start a customer intake.
4. Select several offerings and quantities.
5. Optionally enter a delivery date and instructions.
6. Optionally capture or upload photos/videos.
7. Review snapshotted selections, quantities, prices, and total.
8. Confirm the commercial order and associated Service Job.
9. Find the job in the due-work queue and progress it to delivery.

The prototype should test both the shortest path—with only selected
offerings—and the richer path with date, instructions, media, assignment,
partial readiness, and customer tracking. It must contain no
dry-cleaning-specific database behavior, navigation, required fields, or
business-type checks.

---

Prototype three connected surfaces:

- Dashboard: create a Service and merchant-defined priced offerings.
- Mobile/staff: find or create a customer, select offerings and quantities,
  optionally set due time/instructions/media, review totals, and confirm
  intake.
- Work/customer: manage due jobs, assignment, partial readiness, exceptions,
  and safe public tracking.

Test the shortest path with only an offering and quantity, plus a rich path
with customer, promised date, notes, media, assignment, partial readiness, and
tracking. Include empty, validation, offline, upload-failure, stale-state, and
permission-denied states.

Use dry cleaning only as test data; repeat with a repair or consulting example
to prove generic behavior.

## Resolved Catalog Setup Boundary

- A simple Service creates an implicit default Sellable Variant and one
  fixed-price Service Offering.
- Advanced Service setup uses merchant-defined Variant Option Groups to
  generate combinations with independent fixed or quote-required prices.
- Combinations may be disabled without creating an industry-specific type.
- Service setup never displays or creates Inventory Units, Stock Behavior,
  Opening Stock, balances, or Stock Transformations.

This issue still owns the unresolved intake, work, evidence, due-work, and
customer experience after a Service Offering has been configured.
