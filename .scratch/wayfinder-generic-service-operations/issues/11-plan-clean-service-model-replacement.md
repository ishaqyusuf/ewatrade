## Question

What exact clean-cutover sequence deletes the current Service profile, job,
request, dry-cleaning reader, metadata, API, client-state, route, seed, fixture,
and QA assumptions, resets development data, and introduces only the resolved
generic Service model with no compatibility layer?

## Comments

Use a destructive Service cutover:

1. Remove old Service profiles, jobs, requests, dry-cleaning readers, metadata
   fallbacks, APIs, routes, seeds, fixtures, and offline state.
2. Introduce only the resolved generic Service schema and contracts.
3. Reset disposable development data.
4. Regenerate database clients and apply required migration/push commands.
5. Clear incompatible client caches and queued events.
6. Seed neutral examples only.
7. Verify no industry-specific branch, compatibility adapter, dual write, or
   old status reader remains.

Historical prototype records do not require migration.
