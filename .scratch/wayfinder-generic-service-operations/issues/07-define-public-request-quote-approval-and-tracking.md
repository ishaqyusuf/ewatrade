## Question

What public flows should let a customer request or choose services, receive and
approve a quote, pay a deposit or balance, provide intake details, follow safe
status updates, respond to changes, and track completion without exposing
private evidence, contacts, actors, or raw identifiers?

## Comments

A public Service Request begins as unconfirmed intent and does not create an
Order or Job automatically. Staff can reject it, request clarification, or
produce a Quote. Customer approval converts the accepted quote idempotently
into a Commercial Order and, for tracked lines, a Service Job.

Public access uses an opaque revocable token. Tracking exposes only an
allowlisted business name, public reference, safe milestone, promised time,
approved customer message, and payment action where applicable.

Deposits and balances belong to the Commercial Order/payment boundary. Private
evidence, internal notes, actor identities, contact records, and raw database
IDs are never exposed.

## Approved Pipeline Comment

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: A public Service Request records intent only. Staff may ask
for clarification, decline it, or issue a versioned Quote with expiry and
snapshotted lines. Only acceptance of the current Quote version idempotently
creates a Commercial Order; tracked lines create work according to Work
Authorization Policy. Deposits and balances belong to that Order. Public
Request, Quote approval, and tracking use scoped, opaque, revocable,
rate-limited tokens. Tracking exposes only business identity, public reference,
safe milestone, current promise, approved messages, explicitly published
evidence, and permitted payment action. Public flows belong to the business
storefront host; authenticated operations remain on the shared app host.

Why: Request, commercial proposal, payment, and operational work are different
commitments and need independent lifecycles and privacy boundaries.

Assumptions / risk: Public direct checkout without staff confirmation is not
part of the initial Request/Quote flow.
