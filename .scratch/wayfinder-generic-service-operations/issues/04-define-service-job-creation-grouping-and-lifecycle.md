# Define Service Job Creation, Grouping, And Lifecycle

## Question

When is operational work created, what does one Service Job group, how do
Service Offering lines become work lines/tasks, which lifecycle states and
events are necessary, and how are immediate, tracked, partial, failed, rework,
completed, and cancelled outcomes represented without using payment state?

## Comments

Use the multi-item dry-cleaning intake as a lifecycle acceptance case. The
recommended simple default is one Service Job for one confirmed intake/order,
with each selected Service Offering represented as a work line retaining its
quantity and captured instructions.

The initial generic lifecycle should support received, in progress, ready,
delivered, cancelled, and explicit exception events without using payment state
as work state. Partial completion must be representable when some lines are
ready before others, even if the first UI keeps job-level actions simple.

Splitting one order into multiple jobs should be an explicit operation or later
capability—not an automatic industry-specific rule.

---

Create one Service Job per confirmed order/intake by default, containing only
tracked Service Offering lines. Immediate or charge-only lines do not require a
job. Splitting work into additional jobs is explicit rather than automatic.

Use job states received, in progress, ready, completed, and cancelled.
Represent delay, failure, rework, pickup, and delivery as auditable events or
outcomes. Job lines carry their own progress so partial readiness and partial
completion are possible without forcing the whole job forward.

Commercial payment/refund state remains on the Order and never substitutes for
a work state.

## Approved Pipeline Comment

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: Only tracked Order lines create work. Default one Service Job
for compatible tracked lines in one confirmed Order/Intake, but permit zero to
many Jobs per Order and explicit Job splitting. One Order line may allocate
exact quantities across multiple Job Lines without over-allocation. Job Lines
own `QUEUED`, `IN_PROGRESS`, `BLOCKED`, `READY_FOR_HANDOFF`, `COMPLETED`, or
`CANCELLED` progress; the Job exposes a derived summary including partial
states. Delay, failed attempts, rejection, handoff, and rework are typed events
or exception records. Completed allocations remain immutable; later rework is
linked rather than silently reopened.

Why: Line-authoritative progress supports partial readiness and split work
without turning every industry example into a grouping rule.

Assumptions / risk: Job merge after meaningful work history is not required;
explicit split is the v1 regrouping operation.
