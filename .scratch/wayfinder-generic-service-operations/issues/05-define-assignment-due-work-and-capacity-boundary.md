## Question

What can be assigned, to whom, when; how are promised/due/start/completion times,
priority, delay, rescheduling, queues, appointments, resource capacity, and
multi-worker responsibility modeled; and which scheduling concepts remain
outside the first implementation?

## Comments

Support one optional delivery/due date on the Service Job during the simple
intake flow. The UI may label this “Delivery date (optional),” but the domain
decision must clarify whether it is a customer request, a merchant promise, or
an internal target.

For the first implementation, prefer an optional job-level due date inherited
by its work lines. Record later rescheduling as an auditable event. Appointment
slots, worker capacity planning, and line-specific deadlines should remain
separate unless another accepted scenario requires them immediately.

---

Distinguish an optional customer-requested time from an optional
merchant-promised due time. For direct staff intake, “Delivery date (optional)”
records the promised due time unless staff explicitly marks it as a customer
request.

V1 assignment targets the whole Service Job and references an authorized
business member. Support priority, due/overdue queues, reassignment, and
audited rescheduling. Job-line assignment, multi-worker planning, appointments,
time slots, and resource-capacity optimization are deferred.

Due status must derive from the current promised time and completion state, not
from payment status.

## Approved Pipeline Comment

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: V1 supports one optional primary assignee on the Service Job,
restricted to an active authorized member of the same Business and permitted
Store. Assignment and reassignment are audited. Priority is `NORMAL` or
`URGENT`. Customer-requested time and merchant-promised Due Commitment are
separate. The Job-level promise is inherited by its lines; differently due work
is explicitly split. Rescheduling requires a reason and preserves history;
overdue state is derived from the current promise and incomplete work.

Why: This is enough for an actionable due-work queue without prematurely
building workforce scheduling.

Assumptions / risk: Job-line assignment, teams, multiple workers, appointments,
time slots, equipment/resource capacity, duration optimization, and automatic
scheduling are deferred.
