## Question

How do customers or staff select a Service Offering, express quantity and
options, capture customer/instructions/evidence, distinguish request, quote,
booking, order, and direct intake, calculate/snapshot price, and decide whether
paid or unpaid work should begin?

## Comments

Add this generic acceptance scenario:

A merchant creates a “Dry Cleaning” service with priced offerings such as
Agbada / Small = 400, Agbada / Large = 700, Shirt / Small = 200, and Shirt /
Large = 300. Staff can select multiple offerings and quantities during one
customer intake.

Each selection must resolve to a specific Service Offering and snapshot its
service name, option values, unit price, quantity, and calculated total. The
decision should establish whether values such as garment and size are variant
dimensions, configurable option values, or separately named offerings.

Delivery date, instructions, photos, and videos are optional and must not
prevent a simple intake from being confirmed. “Dry Cleaning” is an example
configuration, not a system or industry type.

---

Resolve merchant-defined option combinations into concrete priced Service
Offerings. For the dry-cleaning acceptance case, garment and size may be
configuration dimensions, but Agbada/Large, Shirt/Small, and every other priced
combination must resolve to a stable offering identity.

Order lines snapshot offering name, option labels, unit price, quantity, and
total. A Service Offering declares whether it is immediate/charge-only or
tracked work. Only tracked lines create operational work.

Staff may confirm direct intake with optional customer, promised date,
instructions, and evidence. Payment state must not decide work state; merchant
policy determines whether unpaid work may begin.

## Approved Pipeline Comment

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: A Service Offering declares `CHARGE_ONLY` or `TRACKED` work
policy and a Work Authorization Policy of Order confirmation, required payment,
or manual release. Merchant option combinations resolve to stable concrete
Service Offerings. Quantities cross boundaries as exact decimal strings with
whole quantity as the default. Direct Intake starts as a Draft and requires
only an Offering and positive quantity; customer, requested time, promised
time, instructions, condition, evidence, and assignment are optional.
Confirmation atomically creates the Commercial Order and tracked work.

Why: This preserves the simple select-items-and-confirm flow while supporting
hourly or measured Services and keeping price/payment facts in Commerce.

Assumptions / risk: Anonymous walk-in Intake cannot use customer tracking or
provider communication until a reachable customer is attached.
