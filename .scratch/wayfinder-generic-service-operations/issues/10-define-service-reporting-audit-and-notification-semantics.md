# Define Service Reporting, Audit, And Notification Semantics

## Question

Which operational, commercial, due/overdue, cycle-time, workload, assignment,
request/quote conversion, cancellation/rework, customer communication, privacy,
and audit reports are authoritative, and which events or snapshots supply each
metric without deriving money from mutable work state?

## Comments

Commercial Orders and payment records are authoritative for revenue,
discounts, paid amounts, and balances. Service Job events are authoritative for
received, in-progress, ready, completed, cancelled, rework, cycle-time,
due/overdue, and assignment metrics.

Reports must use immutable offering/order snapshots and timestamped work events
rather than current mutable names or states. Notification reporting
distinguishes intent creation, manual sharing, provider delivery, and failure.

Every report requires tenant scope and privacy-safe dimensions. Evidence
content is excluded from ordinary reports; audits show metadata and access
events only.

## Approved Pipeline Comment

> *This was generated by AI during Wayfinder issue commenting.*

Proposed answer: Commerce is authoritative for revenue, discounts, refunds,
paid amounts, and balances. Service Work Events, Job Lines, Due Commitments,
Assignments, and exception/rework records are authoritative for work in
progress, throughput, cycle time, overdue, blocked, readiness, cancellation,
rework, and workload. Reports use immutable snapshots and timestamped events.
Evidence content is excluded; audits may expose scoped metadata/access events.
Separate Notification Intent, rendered Customer Message, manual sharing, and
provider Delivery Attempt so retries and failures do not duplicate intent.

Why: Mutable work state cannot safely reconstruct commercial history, and an
intent to notify is not proof that a provider delivered a message.

Assumptions / risk: Provider-specific delivery analytics remain unavailable
until a provider adapter exists.
