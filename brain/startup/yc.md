# Y Combinator Strategy

## Purpose
Document EwaTrade's approach to applying for, preparing for, and maximising value from Y Combinator.

---

## Why YC

- $500K standard deal (most recent batches) for ~7% equity — strong pre-seed capital
- Network: YC alumni network is the world's most valuable startup peer group
- Credibility: YC stamp accelerates fundraising, hiring, and enterprise sales
- Africa/emerging markets: YC has a growing portfolio of African startups (Wave, Paystack's investors include YC-connected funds, Flutterwave alumni, etc.)
- Focus: 3-month sprint format forces clarity on what actually matters

### YC African Portfolio Reference (relevant companies)
- **Paystack** (acquired by Stripe) — Nigerian payments
- **Mono** — financial data infrastructure, Nigeria
- **Kuda** — digital bank, Nigeria
- **Shuttle** — logistics, Nigeria/Africa
- **Sabi** — B2B commerce, Nigeria
- **Omnibiz** — B2B distribution, Nigeria

---

## Application Strategy

### When To Apply
- Apply when you have: a working MVP, at least 2 founders, and early evidence of usage (even unpaid pilots)
- Do NOT wait for perfection — YC backs teams and ideas at very early stages
- Typical application windows: **S batch** (March deadline) | **W batch** (October deadline)
- Check current deadlines at: ycombinator.com/apply

### Who Should Apply
YC strongly prefers **2–3 co-founders**. If EwaTrade has a solo founder, address this proactively in the application. YC has accepted solo founders, but it requires extra justification.

## Application Sections & How To Answer Them

### Company / Product

**"Describe your company in 50 characters or less"**
> Commerce + logistics OS for African merchants

**"What is your company going to make?"**
> EwaTrade is a multi-tenant SaaS platform that gives African small businesses everything they need to run commerce operations: a storefront website, order management, POS, dispatch coordination, and WhatsApp-native AI commerce. We are starting in Nigeria where 40M+ SMEs manage their businesses via WhatsApp, notebooks, and Excel — no integrated software exists for them.

**"Why did you pick this idea to work on?"**
> Focus on direct founder experience with the problem: watching Nigerian merchants manually copy-paste WhatsApp orders, coordinate delivery via phone calls, and lose track of inventory in notebooks. Existing tools (Shopify, WooCommerce) are built for Western merchants — wrong price point, hostile to offline use, no local payment or dispatch integration.

---

### Traction

**"How far along are you?"**
Be specific and honest. Examples of strong signals:
- "We have 8 merchants processing orders on the platform, averaging 12 orders/week each."
- "We've processed ₦5M in GMV in the last 30 days."
- "We have 3 paying merchants at ₦15,000/month each."

> YC cares about **growth rate**, not absolute numbers. "We had 3 merchants in January, 8 in February, 18 in March" is compelling even at small scale.

**"How do you know people want this?"**
> Reference interview evidence: "We interviewed 25 merchants in Lagos markets. 19 said they manage orders manually via WhatsApp. 14 said they'd pay for an integrated solution. 5 became pilot users."

---

### Market

**"Who are your competitors, and what do you understand about your business that they don't?"**

| Competitor | What They Do | Why EwaTrade Wins |
|---|---|---|
| Shopify | Global ecommerce SaaS | Western-built, no offline POS, no dispatch network, USD pricing |
| Bumpa (Nigeria) | Shopify-like for Nigeria | No dispatch network, no POS, no WhatsApp AI |
| Quickteller / Payarena | Payment-first | Not a commerce operating system |
| VendHQ / Loyverse | POS | No storefront, no dispatch, no WhatsApp |
| WhatsApp Business | Messaging | No inventory, no order tracking, no payments |

> Our insight: **African merchants don't need a better Shopify — they need a platform built around WhatsApp and dispatch, which is how commerce actually works here.**

---

### Business Model

**"How do you make money?"**
> Monthly SaaS subscription per merchant (tiered: Basic ₦5,000/mo, Pro ₦15,000/mo, Enterprise custom). Secondary: per-transaction fee on dispatches brokered (~2%), marketplace promotion fees. No wallet, no float — all payments go directly to merchant bank accounts.

**"How much money could you make per year if you had 1,000 merchants?"**
> At average ₦10,000/month per merchant: ₦120M/year (~$80K). With dispatch brokerage at 500 deliveries/merchant/month × 2% of ₦1,000 average fee: additional ₦120M/year. Total: ~₦240M (~$160K ARR at 1K merchants). Scales rapidly with the network — each dispatch request benefits all merchants.

### Founders

**"Why is your team uniquely positioned to win this?"**
> (Document each founder's specific relevant experience — technical depth, African market knowledge, merchant relationships, logistics/payments background)

Key signals YC looks for:
- Founders who deeply understand the problem from personal or professional experience
- Technical co-founder who can build fast
- Domain expertise in the target market (Nigerian commerce, logistics, fintech)
- Demonstrated ability to move fast (shipped MVP, got users)

---

### Video

YC requires a **1-minute founder video**.

Structure:
1. Introduce yourselves (10 sec)
2. What EwaTrade does (15 sec)
3. The problem in concrete terms (15 sec)
4. Your traction / proof (10 sec)
5. Why you are the right team (10 sec)

Tips:
- Film in a quiet, well-lit space
- Speak directly to camera, be natural
- No slides — just founders talking
- Show energy and conviction

## YC Interview Preparation

If invited to interview (either virtual or in-person in San Francisco):

### Format
- 10 minutes, 2–3 YC partners
- Very fast Q&A — they interrupt, push back, probe assumptions
- No formal presentation — pure conversation

### Common Questions

**Product**
- "What exactly does your product do?"
- "Show me the product" (be ready to demo live)
- "What does a merchant do on day 1 after signing up?"

**Market**
- "How big is this market?"
- "Why hasn't anyone built this already?"
- "What's stopping Shopify from adding a dispatch layer?"

**Traction**
- "How many users do you have?"
- "Are they paying?"
- "What's your growth rate month over month?"
- "What's your churn?"

**Business Model**
- "How do you make money?"
- "What's your CAC? What's your LTV?"

**Team**
- "How do you know each other?"
- "Who does what?"
- "What would make you quit?"

**Competition**
- "What is Bumpa doing? How are you different?"
- "What do you have that a well-funded competitor can't copy in 6 months?"

### Preparation Tips
- Know your numbers cold (users, GMV, growth, churn, revenue)
- Practise answering every question in 1–2 sentences (YC partners cut off long answers)
- Prepare a 90-second live demo (one real merchant, one real order)
- Have a sharp "why us" answer for the team question
- Research YC partners who may interview you

## Post-YC Plan

If accepted:
1. **Batch goals**: by Demo Day, target: 50 merchants, ₦50M GMV/month, clear unit economics
2. **Fundraising**: use Demo Day to close seed round ($1M–$3M)
3. **Hiring**: use YC network to find technical co-founders or early engineers
4. **Customer introductions**: leverage YC network for enterprise/B2B pilot introductions
