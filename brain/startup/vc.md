# Venture Capital Strategy

## Purpose
Document EwaTrade's fundraising approach, investor targeting, pitch narrative, and readiness checklist.

---

## Fundraising Thesis

EwaTrade is building the commerce and logistics operating system for African small businesses. We are raising to accelerate product development, grow the merchant base in Nigeria, and build the dispatch network.

The Africa commerce infrastructure market is large, underserved, and fragmented:
- 40M+ SMEs in Nigeria alone
- Majority manage operations via WhatsApp, paper notebooks, and Excel
- No dominant all-in-one platform comparable to Shopify + Toast + a dispatch layer
- Smartphone and mobile payment penetration growing rapidly

---

## Funding Stages

### Pre-Seed (Current / Near-Term Target)
- **Amount**: $150K – $500K
- **Use of funds**:
  - Complete MVP (onboarding, order management, basic POS, dispatch bidding)
  - Fund 3–4 months of pilot program (5–10 merchants)
  - Hire 1–2 engineers
  - Legal/compliance setup (CAC filing, ToC, Privacy Policy)
- **Target investors**: angels, African tech angels, accelerators (YC, Techstars, Founders Factory Africa)
- **Milestone to unlock**: 10 paying merchants, ₦10M GMV/month

### Seed
- **Amount**: $1M – $3M
- **Use of funds**:
  - Scale to 100+ merchants in Lagos
  - Build dispatch network (50+ verified providers)
  - Full website builder + marketplace
  - Mobile app
  - 6–8 person team
- **Milestone to unlock**: 100 paying merchants, ₦100M GMV/month, clear unit economics

### Series A
- **Amount**: $5M – $15M
- **Use of funds**:
  - Expand to 3 cities (Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt) + 1 additional country
  - Full AI WhatsApp commerce
  - Tauri POS desktop rollout
  - B2B/wholesale product
  - 20–30 person team
- **Milestone to unlock**: profitable unit economics, strong retention, network effects visible

## Pitch Narrative Structure

### 1-Line
> "EwaTrade is Shopify + a dispatch network for African merchants — one platform for storefronts, orders, delivery, POS, and WhatsApp commerce."

### Problem
- 40M+ African SMEs run operations on WhatsApp, notebooks, and Excel
- They have no storefront, no dispatch coordination, no inventory tracking
- Existing tools (Shopify, WooCommerce) are built for Western markets — expensive, offline-hostile, no local payment or dispatch integration

### Solution
- Multi-tenant platform: every merchant gets a website, order management, POS, and access to a dispatch network
- WhatsApp-native commerce: customers can order via WhatsApp chat; AI assistant handles the conversation
- Works offline: POS never goes down even without internet
- Payments go directly to merchants — no float, no trust issues

### Market
- Nigeria: 40M+ SMEs, $90B+ retail market, growing mobile commerce penetration
- Africa: 100M+ SMEs — same infrastructure gap exists in Ghana, Kenya, Rwanda, Ethiopia
- SAM: merchants willing to pay for software — estimated 2–5M SMEs in Nigeria alone
- SOM: Lagos-first, 10,000 merchants in Year 1

### Business Model
| Revenue Stream | Description |
|---|---|
| Monthly SaaS subscription | Per-merchant plan (Basic, Pro, Enterprise) |
| Transaction fee (optional) | Small % split on orders (if enabled) |
| Dispatch brokerage fee | Fee per matched delivery request |
| Marketplace listing / promotion | Featured merchant + product placement |
| POS software license | One-time or annual fee for Tauri desktop app |

## Key Metrics Investors Will Ask About

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Monthly Active Merchants (MAM) | Core retention signal |
| GMV (Gross Merchandise Value) | Scale of commerce flowing through platform |
| Take rate | Platform economics |
| Merchant LTV | Revenue ceiling per customer |
| CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) | Unit economics |
| Churn rate | Retention / PMF signal |
| Dispatch orders / month | Network effect proxy |
| Avg orders per merchant / month | Engagement depth |

## Notes
- Lead with traction over vision at pre-seed — even 5 merchants with strong usage beats perfect slides
- Africa-focused VCs want to see Nigeria-first with clear expansion thesis, not pan-Africa from day 1
- Avoid overclaiming: "Shopify for Africa" framing is overused — lean into the dispatch network + WhatsApp differentiation
- Payment model (no float) is a strong trust signal for investors familiar with African fintech risks
